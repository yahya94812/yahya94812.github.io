# My CS Journey Starts Here 🌱

After my PUC, I began exploring programming languages — and dove into the PUC CS books, as I have taken Biology instead of Computer Science. That felt like the wrong decision at the time, and this was my way of filling that gap.

I still remember the day I bought those CS PUC books, carrying them home in the rain. Tech had already pulled me in deeply — not just programming, but electronics too. Around the same time, I was exploring transistors and working on my own electronics projects alongside my coding.

I remember setting up Python IDLE on Windows 7 and printing my very first `print` statement, following a YouTube channel called **Telusko**, which taught me my first programming language. Back then, I had just one goal: write a program that could print the full multiplication table for any number I entered. It felt impossible at first — but by around 6:00 PM that day, I'd finally cracked it.
