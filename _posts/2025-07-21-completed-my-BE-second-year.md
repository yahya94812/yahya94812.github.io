# Completed My BE Second Year 🎯

Second year ended on a high note — I secured an **exceptional 9.2 SGPA**, and along with that, I learned a ton.

**Technical highlights:**
- Deepened my understanding of **C and C++**, especially pointers
- Learned **Assembly language**, which really showed me how a computer works under the hood. I had zero prior experience writing assembly to control hardware, but I built a solid understanding through my lab practicals — and I still remember the moment I rotated a motor for the first time during my final lab exam. That one stuck with me.
- Went deeper into **Python** by following Apna College on YouTube
- Started exploring **JavaScript** and diving into **DSA**
- Most importantly, I finally learned how **Git** and **GitHub** actually work — took me about a month to properly wrap my head around it
- Switched to **Linux (Ubuntu)** for better performance on my old laptop. It took about 2 months to get fully comfortable, but I came out the other side a huge Linux fan — *"I use Linux, by the way!"*

**The hackathon experience:**
I took part in my first hackathon, where our team built **MindCare AI** using the Gemini API — brand new at the time. I still remember the chaos of everyone's ChatGPT limits running out mid-hackathon, and us switching to Claude across multiple accounts just to keep building. We didn't win, but the experience itself was worth it.

Later, I won my **college-level DSA/problem-solving hackathon**, taking home ₹3000 as a reward — a nice full-circle moment after the first one didn't go as planned.
